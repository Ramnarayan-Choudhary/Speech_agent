\begin{abstract}
We present an end-to-end speech-to-speech agent designed for low-resource Indian languages, specifically Marathi, Gujarati, and English. The system chains five neural components into a single inference loop: Silero voice activity detection for silence trimming, a novel domain-constrained language identification scheme that restricts Whisper's decoder logits to the three target languages, per-language LoRA adapters dynamically switched at runtime on a shared Whisper-Large-v3-Turbo backbone, an LLM-based translation layer powered by Qwen3-14B that corrects phonetic transcription errors and produces fluent English output, and AI4Bharat's Indic Parler-TTS for multilingual speech synthesis. Each LoRA adapter adds only 113\,MB (7\% of the full model) and trains in roughly 30 minutes on a single GPU with just 160 samples. On the Google FLEURS benchmark, the system achieves a word error rate of 25.6\% for English, 72.1\% for Marathi, and 75.0\% for Gujarati---with the high Indic numbers largely attributable to domain mismatch between the IndicSpeech training data and FLEURS test conditions, as in-domain validation yields approximately 29\% WER. The LLM translation layer reaches a BLEU score of 0.59 for Marathi-to-English, and the full pipeline processes audio at roughly three times real-time on an NVIDIA RTX 5000 Ada GPU. We provide ablation studies examining the effect of LoRA fine-tuning on hallucination suppression, the in-domain versus out-of-distribution accuracy gap, and the per-stage latency profile. All code and trained adapters are publicly available.
\end{abstract}
